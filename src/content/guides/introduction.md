## Early Game Priorities

Your immediate goal is survival, which means focusing on:

- **Reach 6 <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/32px-Av_symbol.png" alt="AV">AV</span> (Armor Value)**: equip armor in every available slot to reach 6 AV. This critical threshold prevents most early-game threats from overwhelming you. Early on, one point of AV equals roughly three points of <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/32px-Dv_symbol.png" alt="DV">DV</span> (dodge value), so don't worry about minor dodge penalties from armor.
- **Weapon Strategy**: equip both ranged and melee weapons. Since both recommended builds have night vision, you can use <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Twohanded_steel_war_hammer.png" alt="two-handed">Two-handed</span> weapons without needing a free hand for light sources. These provide excellent early-game damage output. Keep ranged weapons to soften tough enemies before they reach you.
- **Resource Management**: maintain at least 30 to 50 drams of water. Never drop water to carry more loot; trade efficiently using lightweight valuables and drop items you want to sell near merchants for later use when merchants restock with the items you need.

## Your First Steps in Joppa

The in-game tutorial has taught you the basics of movement, combat, and survival. Now that you are in <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_joppa.png" alt="Joppa">Joppa</span>, let's turn those basics into long-term survival skills. Take some time to explore Joppa and get oriented. Talk to the villagers, especially:

- <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Mehmet.png" alt="Mehmet">Mehmet</span>: found near the watervine fields in central Joppa. He gives you the first quest, *What's Eating the Watervine?* His quest sets you on your initial adventure and introduces you to <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_red_rock.png" alt="Red Rock">Red Rock</span>.
- <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Argyve.png" alt="Argyve">Argyve</span>: a tinker in the bottom-left corner who trades <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/32px-Yal_unidentified.png" alt="artifact">artifacts</span> and <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Data_disk.png" alt="data disk">data disks</span>. He offers several important quests that advance the main storyline. He'll attack the village's Mechanimist convert on sight, so handle related business with them first.
- <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Nima_ruda.png" alt="Nima Ruda">Nima Ruda</span>: the village apothecary, who sells essential healing items. Look for her and stock up on <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Witchwood_bark.png" alt="witchwood bark">witchwood bark</span>. Be aware that it may confuse you, so find a safe spot before healing.
- <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Watervine_farmer_and_mechanimist_convert.png" alt="Mechanimist convert">Mechanimist convert</span>: a farmer who provides information about the <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_six_day_stilt.png" alt="Six Day Stilt">Six Day Stilt</span>. Speak with them early, before they encounter Argyve. They'll give you a quest, *O Glorious Shekhinah!*, and <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/32px-Trihologram_bracelet_unidentified.png" alt="trinket">a small trinket</span>.
- <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Tam_dromad_merchant.png" alt="Tam">Tam</span>: the dromad merchant in the southeast corner. Your primary source for essential equipment like ammunition, weapons, and armor. His stock refreshes regularly, making him valuable for early game supplies. Keep an eye out for his <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Musket.png" alt="musket">musket</span> and <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Lead_slug.png" alt="lead slug">lead slugs</span>.

You start with limited resources, but the village offers some opportunities for self-help. <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Chest.png" alt="chest">Chests</span> in villager homes can contain useful gear. Be careful, though: most buildings have fence walls that allow villagers to see inside even with doors closed. The lower-left hut closest to your starting position is an exception. You can safely loot its chest when the door is closed, and it often contains a jug with water. For other buildings, ensure no one is watching before opening chests you do not own. Since both recommended builds come with night vision and bleeding resistance, you can focus on finding armor and weapons instead of worrying about <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Floating_glowsphere.png" alt="floating glowsphere">light sources</span> or <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Bandage.png" alt="bandage">bandages</span>.

Travel one zone north of <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_joppa.png" alt="Joppa">Joppa</span> to find an ancient statue. Examining it reveals a historic site, an important early-game dungeon with valuable rewards after clearing it. Always read books, examine statues, and inspect engraved or painted items. These often reveal historic sites and useful information about Qud's world.

## Combat Essentials

Check enemies before fighting by looking at them. **Average** means dangerous; fight them one-on-one, never in groups. For <span class="ent-red">Very Tough</span> enemies, it's better to run. **Legendary** creatures are especially dangerous; running from these is also smarter.

Position yourself wisely. This is crucial for survival. Use doorways and terrain to face only one enemy at a time. When you see an enemy, return to already explored areas rather than unknown territory. Let enemies come to you when they're an odd number of tiles away. This prevents them from getting a free hit.

Start fights at full health since injuries accumulate quickly. The `Alt` key helps you identify hostile creatures. Keep Sprint ready to escape dangerous situations. Many deaths happen from fighting when retreat is the wiser choice.

See some fighting strategies around <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_red_rock.png" alt="Red Rock">Red Rock</span> from the recommended builds. Note that baboons initially run away from you but come towards you eventually.

## <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_red_rock.png" alt="Red Rock">Red Rock</span> (Quest: *What's Eating the Watervine?*)

### Surface Preparation

Clear the baboons in zones surrounding Red Rock before venturing underground. This accomplishes two goals:

- Gains crucial early experience
- Creates a safe retreat path for underground exploration

### Underground Threats

The quest target, <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Girshling.png" alt="Girshling">Girshling</span>, lives four levels deep in the cave. On your way down, you'll face common enemies like <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Bear.png" alt="Bear">Bears</span>, <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Snapjaw_scavenger.png" alt="Snapjaw scavenger">Snapjaws</span>, <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Giant_amoeba.png" alt="Giant amoeba">Giant amoebas</span>, <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Horned_chameleon.png" alt="Horned chameleon">Horned chameleons</span>, and <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Giant_centipede.png" alt="Giant centipede">Giant centipedes</span>. These enemies become easy to handle once you've gained a few levels and followed the combat essentials.

Watch out for rusty areas marked by <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Qudzu.png" alt="Qudzu">Qudzu</span> plants. Rust damages your equipment and reduces its trade value. You can repair rusted items with <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Fixit_spray_foam_identified.png" alt="Fix-It spray foam">Fix-It spray foam</span> or the **Repair** skill.

After getting the <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Girshling_corpse.png" alt="Girshling corpse">girshling corpse</span>, return to <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Elder_irudad.png" alt="Elder Irudad">Elder Irudad</span> in <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_joppa.png" alt="Joppa">Joppa</span>. Save the Fix-it spray foam from your reward. You'll need it for the next quest.

## <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_rust_wells.png" alt="Rust Wells">Rust Wells</span> (Quest: *Weirdwire Conduit... Eureka!*)

### Preparation

First, complete Argyve's two starter quests by giving him any two artifacts. Even broken ones work. You can use the trinket from the Mechanimist convert and another artifact you'll likely find during the *What's Eating the Watervine?* quest.

Before heading to Rust Wells, search for Argyve's former apprentice. Their <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Girshling_corpse.png" alt="flattened remains">flattened remains</span>, containing broken <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Mechanical_wings_identified.png" alt="mechanical wings">mechanical wings</span>, always appear in the center zone of a <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Salt_marsh.png" alt="salt marsh">salt marsh</span> near <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_joppa.png" alt="Joppa">Joppa</span>. Check these center zones until you find them. Repair the wings with Fix-it spray foam or pay Argyve with water to fix them. Wear them to gain flight ability.

### Completion

You may encounter a <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Slugsnout.png" alt="Slugsnout">Slugsnout</span> in the surrounding area when traveling to the Rust Wells. If you do, be aware of their dangerous ranged attacks. You can use walls for cover to force them into melee range where they're weaker, or retreat to safer ground based on your character's defenses and combat experience.

The Rust Wells lies east of <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Ovw_joppa.png" alt="Joppa">Joppa</span>. While some <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Wire_strand.png" alt="wire">wire</span> appears on the surface, the main prize is in central <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Chest_(rare).png" alt="chest">chests</span> within each well. There are three wells, and you need 200 wires to complete the quest.

Start by walking down the well from the edge. When you spot hostile enemies, draw them toward your position near the edge. Then use your mechanical wings to retreat, but do this carefully. Fly up just one level at a time. The wings have a 5% chance of falling, so staying close to the ground is crucial. The previous apprentice's fate shows why this matters. Once enemies have left the center area, circle around and approach the central <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Chest_(rare).png" alt="chest">chest</span> from the opposite, now-clear side to collect the wires and other valuable items. While flying, you can also escape to the world map from any surface tile, making it a perfect emergency escape option.

## Skills

### Early Priority Skills

The **Pistol** skill tree, up through *Disarming Shot*, provides essential combat advantages. Disarming enemies and turrets not only neutralizes threats but also yields valuable weapons for trade. Follow this with basic **Tinkering**: *Disassemble* gives you bits for crafting, while *Tinker I* opens up equipment modifications.

### Defensive Skills

The **Acrobatics** and **Endurance** trees provide crucial defensive bonuses that significantly improve survival through improvements to both AV and DV:

- **Spry**: +2 DV bonus
- **Tumble**: +1 DV and improved Juke
- **Calloused**: +1 AV without DV penalty

### Navigation

From the **Wayfaring** tree, two key Wilderness Lore skills help prevent getting lost in critical areas:

- <span class="ent-location">Salt Dunes</span>: essential for Stilt travel
- <span class="ent-location">Jungle</span>: common terrain type

Other biome skills remain optional; you can navigate those regions safely by walking in familiar terrain.

### Combat Skills

Your early weapon skills should align with your preferences and what you discover during exploration. While the recommended builds favor Single Weapon Fighting, both paths are viable:

- **Single Weapon Fighting**: focuses expertise on one weapon type for maximum effectiveness.
- **Multiweapon Fighting**: works well with various combinations like axes, short blades, long blades, and cudgels.

Start with basic weapon skills and experiment before specializing. The **Tactics** tree enhances either approach with valuable combat maneuvers.

### Support Skills

The **Physic** tree, particularly *Nostrums*, adds essential healing capabilities:

- Treat multiple conditions at campfires
- Heal both yourself and companions
- Handle bleeding, poison, illness, and disease

### Skills Through Reputation

Instead of spending skill points, these important skills can be learned through faction relationships:

- **Barathrumites**: *Tinkering*
- **Kyakukya Mayor**: *Cooking*
- **Six Day Stilt Warden**: *Shield* (while Shield is also available from Yd Freehold, *Wardens Esther* at the Stilt provides easier access early in the game)
- <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Oddlyhued_glowpad.png" alt="Oddly-hued Glowpad">Oddly-hued Glowpad</span>: *Snake Oiler* (always located in the center zone of a random salt marsh parasang; the entire parasang will be covered in dense <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Brinestalk.png" alt="Brinestalk">brinestalk</span>)
- **High Priest of the Stilt**: *Proselytize* (useful for getting items freely from merchants; wait to learn it until completing the *Decoding the Signal* quest, otherwise you'll need significant rep grinding to regain favored status with Mechanimists)

## Factions

Your reputation with different factions shapes how Qud's inhabitants react to you. Understanding these dynamics helps navigate the world safely while building useful alliances.

### Reputation Ranges and Effects

At neutral standing (-249 to 249), most creatures tolerate your presence unless provoked. Falling to **disliked** status (-599 to -250) turns neutral creatures hostile, while becoming **despised** (below -600) makes the entire faction attack on sight. Positive reputation offers benefits: **favored** status (250 to 599) ensures peaceful interactions, while reaching **revered** (above 600) turns faction members into combat allies.

Early game success relies on maintaining neutral standing (-249 to 249) with most groups while cultivating specific beneficial relationships. Consider each interaction's impact on faction dynamics before acting.

### Important Factions

Some factions in Qud deserve special attention as your character develops. These relationships shape your experience in the mid and late game:

- Fellowship of Wardens
- Insects
- Mechanimists
- Naphtaali Tribe
- Oozes
- Robots
- Trolls
- Unshelled Reptiles
- Winged Mammals

Building positive relations with these factions early prevents conflicts that become difficult to manage as you explore more dangerous territories.

> **Trolls** represent a distinctive faction whose threat level diminishes as you grow stronger, yet their value lies primarily in their knowledge of legendary artifacts. While they possess valuable secrets (notably the location of <span class="chip"><img src="/assets/Stopsvalinn_identified.png" alt="Stopsvalinn">Stopsvalinn</span>), don't risk dangerous encounters or kill things unnecessarily to gain their favor, as the rewards rarely justify the effort. Consider their artifacts a potential long-term goal rather than an early-game priority.

## Common Early Game Mistakes

Many new players die repeatedly from these mistakes:

- Fighting multiple enemies at once: even Average enemies become deadly in groups
- Neglecting escape options: plan your retreat before engaging
- Undervaluing armor: reaching 6 AV should be your priority
- Running low on water: drops below 30 drams can be fatal
- Exploring too far too soon: the East is far more dangerous
- Engaging <span class="ent-red">Very Tough</span> enemies: when you see this rating, run

## Final Thoughts

Success in Qud comes gradually. Each character's death teaches valuable lessons, and every run reveals new strategies and secrets. The guidance here covers essential survival tactics, but discovering your own approach to the game's challenges is part of the experience.

Remember that even seasoned players regularly start new characters. Death is not failure but an integral part of exploring Qud's mysteries. Take your time, learn from each run, and don't hesitate to try different builds as you develop your understanding of the game.

**Live and drink, traveler.**
